RUNNING FROM MY RESPONSIBILITIES

  • I think I keep postponing everything
    because finishing things means facing myself again.
    I’ve been running.
    No, not the jog in the park
    but the silent sprint away from my unread emails,
    the piled-up dishes,
    and the voice in my head that keeps saying,
    “You’re falling behind.”
    I rearranged my bookshelf instead of doing the thing.
    I rearranged my pens.
    I made tea I didn’t drink.
    And told myself
    this is productivity too.
    And when I woke up,
    the sun had moved across the room
    like it, too, wanted nothing to do with me.
    But I know the truth
    I am hiding inside small rituals,
    because the big ones scare me.
    The showing up,
    the facing things,
    the trying when I already feel too tired
    it all feels like asking my lungs
    to breathe underwater.
    I tell myself I’ll start tomorrow.
    Tomorrow is my religion
    always holy, waiting,
    always forgiving me
    for the things I didn’t do today.
    There’s some kind of art in avoidance.
    A poetry in pretending.
    A rhythm in saying
    “I’m just tired,”
    when I’m really just scared
    to show up,
    to fail,
    to be seen.
    My responsibilities knock like polite strangers.
    And I hide like a child behind the couch,
    holding my breath,
    hoping they leave a note and go.
    Still,
    for now,
    I lie still,
    making friends with the dust on my desk
    the undone,
    the untouched,
    the undone again.
    I promise I’ll show up.
    Just…
    not today.
    Maybe tomorrow, I’ll finally water the plant.
    Maybe I’ll write back.
    Maybe I’ll stop hiding.
    I keep running from my responsibilities,
    but they always know my address.
